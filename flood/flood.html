Safety and Control Measures During Floods:


A] Before a Flood:



  • Stay informed: Monitor weather reports and listen to local authorities for flood warnings and instructions.

  • Prepare your home:

    • Elevate important belongings and furniture to higher levels.
    • Seal basements and crawlspaces to prevent water intrusion.
    • Install check valves in sewer drains to prevent backflow.
    • Sandbag low-lying doorways and openings.
    • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, first-aid supplies, medications, flashlight, and battery-powered radio.

  • Develop an evacuation plan:

    • Identify evacuation routes and designated shelters.
    • Practice your evacuation plan with your family, including pets, and agree on a meeting location in case you get separated.


B] During a Flood:



  • Evacuate immediately if instructed to do so by authorities or if your home is in danger of flooding.
  • Do not drive through flooded areas: Six inches of moving water can knock you off your feet. Use designated evacuation routes and shelters.
  • Stay away from power lines and electrical equipment: Floodwater can conduct electricity, posing a serious danger.
  • Turn off utilities at the main switch if you evacuate and only if it is safe to do so.
  • Stay on higher ground: If you cannot evacuate, move to upper floors or the roof and wait for rescue.


C] After a Flood:



  • Wait for the water to recede completely before returning home.
  • Be cautious of damaged structures: Buildings and foundations may be weakened by floodwaters.
  • Beware of electrical hazards: Do not touch electrical equipment until it has been inspected by a qualified professional.
  • Report broken gas lines: Do not attempt to turn off the gas yourself.
  • Document the damage: Take pictures and videos of the damage for insurance purposes.
  • Contact your insurance company: File a claim to start the recovery process.
  • Be aware of potential health risks: Floodwater can be contaminated with sewage and other pollutants. Wear protective clothing and boots when cleaning up and avoid contact with floodwater.


D] Additional Control Measures:



  • Community-level measures:
    • Implement flood control measures like levees, flood walls, and channel improvements.
    • Maintain drainage systems and clear debris from waterways.
    • Promote sustainable land management practices to reduce soil erosion and runoff.

  • Individual preparedness: Purchase flood insurance, especially if you live in a flood-prone area. Consider waterproofing your basement or crawlspace. Install flood detection and alarm systems.
